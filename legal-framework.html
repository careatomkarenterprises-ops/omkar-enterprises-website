Key Clauses Explained

Understanding the critical components of our agreement

Clause 2.1: Fixed Return Obligation

Page 2, Section 2.1

"The Borrower shall pay the Lender a fixed contractual return of two and a half percent (2.5%) per month on the principal amount for the duration of this Agreement."

What This Means:

This is our core commitment. Unlike discretionary or performance-dependent returns, the 2.5% monthly payment is contractually obligated. We are legally bound to pay this amount regardless of market conditions or our investment performance.

Why It's Important:
  • Provides predictable income for lenders
  • Creates legally enforceable obligation
  • Establishes clear payment expectations

Clause 2c: Security Mechanism

Page 2, Section 2c

"As security for the Loan, the Borrower has post-dated cheques (PDCs) drawn on 'MR. [LENDER NAME]' for the principal amount. The said cheques shall be enforceable only in the event of continuous non-payment for a period exceeding 90 days."

What This Means:

We provide post-dated cheques as tangible security for your investment. These cheques are drawn for the principal amount and can be presented for payment under the Negotiable Instruments Act if we default on payments for more than 90 consecutive days.

Why It's Important:
  • Provides legal recourse under NI Act
  • 90-day grace period for business flexibility
  • Clear enforcement conditions

Annexure B.33: Dispute Resolution

Annexure B, Clause 33

"All disputes shall be resolved by arbitration in Pune, Maharashtra under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. The arbitral award shall be final and binding on both parties."

What This Means:

Any disagreements or disputes arising from this agreement must first go through arbitration in Pune. This provides a faster, more private, and typically less expensive resolution process compared to traditional court proceedings.

Why It's Important:
  • Faster resolution than court cases
  • Private proceedings
  • Cost-effective for both parties
  • Specific venue (Pune) for clarity

Annexure B.16: Limited Liability

Annexure B, Clause 16

"No claims against director, family members, or personal assets. Liability is limited to company assets only."

What This Means:

Your claims as a lender are limited to the assets of Omkar Enterprises. You cannot make claims against the personal assets of directors, their family members, or unrelated businesses. This creates professional separation between business and personal liabilities.

Why It's Important:
  • Protects personal assets of directors
  • Clear business entity separation
  • Professional lending relationship

Annexure B.11: Grace Period

Annexure B, Clause 11

"The Borrower shall be entitled to a grace period of up to ninety (90) days from the due date to complete repayment, without such delay being deemed a default."

What This Means:

We have up to 90 days from any payment due date to make payments without triggering default conditions. This recognizes business realities like temporary cash flow issues, banking delays, or operational challenges while maintaining our commitment to repay.

Why It's Important:
  • Realistic business planning
  • Mutual understanding of challenges
  • Predictable timelines for resolution

Regulatory Compliance & Position

Understanding our legal standing and regulatory framework

Not a Deposit Scheme

Our agreements are private loan contracts between consenting parties, not public deposits under the Banning of Unregulated Deposit Schemes Act, 2019.

Not RBI-Regulated

We are not an NBFC and do not accept public deposits requiring RBI registration under the RBI Act, 1934.

Not SEBI-Regulated

This is not a collective investment scheme requiring SEBI registration under SEBI (CIS) Regulations.

Bilateral Agreement

Each partnership is governed by a specific bilateral loan agreement between Omkar Enterprises and individual lenders.

Our Compliance Framework

Omkar Enterprises operates as a proprietary investment firm that enters into private loan agreements with individuals. Our compliance is built on:

Formal Documentation: Written agreement for every transaction
Individual Suitability: Personal consultations before agreement
Full Disclosure: Complete transparency of terms and risks
Legal Framework: Enforceable agreement with security mechanisms
Business Registration: Proper GST and business registrations

Dispute Resolution Process

Clear, structured approach to resolving any disagreements

1

Written Notice

Any dispute must begin with written notice via registered post or official email, detailing the specific issue.

2

30-Day Resolution Period

Both parties have 30 days to resolve the matter amicably through discussion and negotiation.

3

Arbitration Initiation

If unresolved, either party may initiate arbitration proceedings in Pune as per the agreement.

4

Arbitration Award

The arbitrator's decision is final and binding, enforceable as a court decree.

Arbitration Specifics

  • Venue: Pune, Maharashtra
  • Governing Law: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996
  • Language: English
  • Costs: Each party bears own costs unless arbitrator decides otherwise
  • Timeline: Typically faster than court proceedings

Risk Acknowledgement

Important considerations for potential lenders

Capital Risk

Your principal investment is at risk. While secured by PDCs and contractual obligations, there is no guarantee of full repayment.

Liquidity Risk

Funds are locked in for minimum 8 months. Early withdrawal is subject to recalculation and penalties.

Market Risk

Returns depend on our investment performance. While fixed returns are contractual, they rely on our business success.

Enforcement Risk

While PDCs provide legal recourse, realization may involve time and costs.

Important Note

This is a private lending arrangement with Omkar Enterprises. It is NOT a bank deposit, NOT insured, NOT regulated by RBI/SEBI, and NOT a guaranteed return scheme. We strongly recommend:

  • Reviewing the complete agreement with your legal advisor
  • Understanding all risks before committing funds
  • Investing only what you can afford to risk
  • Diversifying your investment portfolio
