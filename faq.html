Investment Basics

Omkar Enterprises enters into private loan agreements with individuals. We are NOT a bank, NOT an NBFC, NOT an investment fund. We are a business that:

  • Borrows funds from individuals through documented loan agreements
  • Deploys funds in our business activities (typically proprietary trading and investments)
  • Returns capital with interest as per agreed terms

This is a direct lending arrangement between you (the lender) and Omkar Enterprises (the borrower), governed by a legally binding Loan Agreement.

Important: This is NOT a bank deposit, NOT insured, NOT regulated by RBI/SEBI as a deposit-taking entity.

We have three investment tiers with different minimum amounts:

Foundation Tier

Minimum: ₹10,00,000

Ideal for: First-time partners testing the waters

Growth Tier

Minimum: ₹25,00,000

Ideal for: Serious investors seeking balanced returns

Premier Tier

Minimum: ₹50,00,000

Ideal for: Sophisticated investors maximizing returns

Note: All amounts must be transferred as a single lump sum. We do not accept staggered investments or SIP-type arrangements.

To enter into a lending partnership with Omkar Enterprises, you must meet these criteria:

Legal Capacity

Indian resident above 18 years with valid KYC documents

Able to bear complete loss of principal without financial hardship

Not suitable for: Those who need funds for essential expenses, retirees dependent on investment income, or anyone who cannot tolerate capital loss.

Payment Process:

  1. Monthly Interest: Paid via Post-Dated Cheque (PDC) on 1st of each month
  2. Principal Repayment: Returned at maturity (13th month) via PDC
  3. Reinvestment: Can reinvest for another 13-month cycle

Important: Returns depend on Omkar Enterprises' business performance. These are contractual returns but NOT guaranteed. Past performance doesn't indicate future results.

Example Calculation:

Scenario: ₹10 lakhs invested, withdrawn after 10 months

  • Agreed Return: 2.5% monthly = ₹25,000/month
  • Early Withdrawal Return: 1.5% monthly = ₹15,000/month
  • Difference: ₹10,000/month × 10 months = ₹1,00,000 less
  • Total Received: Principal + ₹1,50,000 (instead of ₹2,50,000)

Important: This investment is NOT liquid. Only invest money you won't need for at least 13 months.

NOT Guaranteed Returns

Returns depend on Omkar Enterprises' business performance. If our investments perform poorly, we may not generate sufficient returns to pay as agreed.

Contractually Fixed Rate

The interest rate (2.5%, 2.7%, or 2.9% monthly) is fixed in the Loan Agreement and doesn't change with market conditions.

Legal Obligation

While not "guaranteed," we have a legal obligation to pay as per agreement. Non-payment constitutes default with legal consequences.

Post-Dated Cheque (PDC) Security:

Legal Instrument

Cheques are negotiable instruments under Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881

Presumption of Debt

Issuing cheque creates presumption of legally enforceable debt

Fast-Track Remedy

Cheque bounce cases under Section 138 have summary proceedings

Criminal Liability

Dishonour can lead to criminal prosecution, not just civil recovery

Enforcement Process for Dishonoured Cheque:

  1. Cheque Presented & Dishonoured

    Bank returns cheque with "insufficient funds" or other reason

  2. Legal Notice (Within 30 days)

    Send demand notice to drawer within 30 days of dishonour

  3. Wait Period (15 days)

    Drawer has 15 days to make payment after receiving notice

  4. File Complaint (Within 30 days)

    If no payment, file complaint under Section 138 within next 30 days

  5. Court Proceedings

    Summary trial, punishable with imprisonment up to 2 years or fine up to twice cheque amount

Limitations & Realities:

  • Time-Consuming: Cases typically take 2-3 years minimum
  • Jurisdiction: Case must be filed where cheque was presented for payment
  • Defenses Available: Drawer can raise various defenses
  • Recovery Not Guaranteed: Even if case wins, recovery depends on drawer's assets
  • Costs: Legal fees, court costs, time investment required

Practical Advice:

While PDCs provide legal recourse, they should not be considered "guaranteed payment." They are a tool for enforcement, but the primary security is our business success and commitment to honoring agreements.

Faster Resolution

Typically faster than court proceedings (6-18 months vs 3-5 years)

Confidential

Proceedings are private, not public like court cases

Expert Arbitrators

Parties can choose arbitrator with relevant expertise

Cost-Effective

Generally less expensive than prolonged court battles

Arbitration Process:

  1. Dispute Arises

    Either party notifies other of dispute in writing

  2. Negotiation (30 days)

    Parties attempt to resolve amicably within 30 days

  3. Arbitration Notice

    If unresolved, either party can invoke arbitration clause

  4. Arbitrator Appointment

    Single arbitrator mutually agreed upon, or appointed by court

  5. Arbitration Proceedings

    Hearings, evidence submission, arguments

  6. Award

    Arbitrator's decision is binding and enforceable like court decree

Jurisdiction Details:

  • Venue: Pune, Maharashtra
  • Language: Proceedings in English
  • Governing Law: Indian Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996
  • Arbitrator Qualifications: Retired judge or legal expert with 10+ years experience
  • Costs: Each party bears own costs unless arbitrator decides otherwise

Your Legal Rights:

Despite arbitration clause, you retain certain rights:

  • Right to challenge arbitrator appointment for bias
  • Right to appeal arbitration award on limited grounds
  • Right to approach courts for interim relief (injunctions, asset freezing)
  • Right to file criminal complaint for cheque bounce (separate from arbitration)

TDS Calculation Example:

Scenario: ₹10,00,000 investment at 2.5% monthly

  • Monthly Interest: ₹25,000
  • TDS @10%: ₹2,500 deducted each month
  • Net Received: ₹22,500 per month
  • Annual TDS: ₹30,000 (₹2,500 × 12)
  • Annual Interest: ₹3,00,000 (₹25,000 × 12)
Claiming TDS Credit:

The TDS deducted appears in your Form 26AS. You can claim credit while filing ITR:

  1. Download Form 26AS from Income Tax portal
  2. Verify TDS entries match Form 16A from us
  3. Include interest income in "Income from Other Sources"
  4. Claim TDS credit in "Taxes Paid" section
  5. If tax liability higher than TDS, pay balance tax
  6. If tax liability lower, claim refund
